Bengali Kacchi Biryani Recipe

Ingredients:

  • For the Meat Marinade:
    • Mutton (or beef) - 1.5 kg (bone-in, medium pieces)
    • Plain yogurt - 1 cup
    • Raw papaya paste - 2 tbsp (acts as tenderizer)
    • Ginger paste - 2 tbsp
    • Garlic paste - 2 tbsp
    • Red chili powder - 1 tbsp (adjust to taste)
    • Turmeric powder - ½ tsp
    • Garam masala powder - 1 tsp
    • Mace and nutmeg powder - ½ tsp
    • Cinnamon sticks - 2-3
    • Green cardamoms - 5-6
    • Cloves - 5-6
    • Bay leaves 0 2
    • Salt - to taste
    • Mustard oil - ½ cup
    • Ghee - 3 tbsp
    • Fried onions (beresta) - 1.5 cups
  • For the Rice:
    • Basmati or Kalijeera rice - 1 kg
    • Whole garam masala (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves) - a few pieces
    • Salt - to taste
    • Water - as needed
    • Kewra water - 1 tbsp (optional)
    • Rose water - 1 tbsp (optional)
    • Saffron - few strands soaked in warm milk (optional, or yellow food color)

Instuctions:

  1. Marinate the Meat (4–8 hours or overnight):
    • Wash and drain the meat.
    • Mix it with all the marinade ingredients: yogurt, papaya paste, spices, oil, ghee,
      salt, and half of the fried onions (beresta).
    • Cover and marinate in the refrigerator.
  2. Prepare the Rice:
    • Wash the rice and soak for 30 minutes.
    • Boil water in a large pot, add salt and whole garam masala.
    • Cook rice until it's about 70% done (still has a bite), then drain and set aside.
  3. Assemble the Biryani:
    • In a large heavy-bottomed pot (or handi), spread the marinated raw meat at the bottom.
    • Layer the semi-cooked rice on top of the meat evenly.
    • Sprinkle remaining beresta, ghee, kewra water, rose water, and saffron milk (or food color)
      over the rice.
    • Seal the lid with dough or use a tight-fitting lid to trap the steam ("dum" cooking method).
  4. Cook on Dum:
    • Place the pot over high heat for 10-15 minutes to generate steam.
    • Then reduce to low heat and cook for 1.5 to 2 hours.
    • You can place a griddle or tawa under the pot to prevent burning.
  5. To Serve:
    • Serve hot with raita, salad, or a boiled egg and aloo (potato) pieces
      (very traditional in Dhaka-style biryani).
